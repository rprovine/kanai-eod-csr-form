**Root Causes:**
1. **Workiz "Total" includes tax and tips ($3,063.47)** — The Workiz sales export bundles tax and tips into the Total column. The field supervisors enter base price only. This accounts for most of the apparent gap when comparing Workiz's $54,365 raw number against EOD reports.
2. **Job #10675 (Wilfred Morita) — multi-day job timing** — Workiz shows the full $6,480 in this week's export because the job was completed this week. However, $4,690 was billed and reported in a prior week's EOD. The $1,790 entered this week represents only the completion visit. The EOD is correct — Workiz is double-counting revenue across weeks.
3. **Multi-crew jobs create reporting complexity** — Job #10688 (Lectie Batula, $7,525 base) was worked by all 5 crew members. Three separate team lead reports on 3/9 total $8,294.66, which covers this job plus others from prior scheduling periods.
